Chicken Cobbler

Description

Chicken Cobbler is a delicious twist on the classic fruit cobbler, combining tender pieces of chicken, vegetables, and a rich, savory gravy topped with golden, fluffy biscuits. This comforting and hearty dish is the perfect comfort food for any occasion, blending savory flavors and flaky biscuits in a delightful culinary creation.

Ingredients

For the Filling

  • 4 Cups Cooked Chicken, Diced or Shredded
  • 2 Tablespoons Butter
  • 1 Onion, Finely Chopped
  • 2 Carrots, Diced
  • 2 Celery Stalks, Chopped
  • 2 Cloves Garlic, Minced
  • 1 Cup Frozen Peas
  • 1 Cup Frozen Corn
  • 1/4 Cup All-Purpose Flour
  • 2 Cups Chicken Broth
  • 1 Cup Whole Milk
  • 1 tsp Dried Thyme
  • Salt and Pepper to Taste
  • Fresh Parsley, Chopped for Garnish

For the Topping

  • 2 Cups All-Purpose Flour
  • 2 tsp Baking Powder
  • 1/2 tsp Baking Soda
  • 1/2 tsp Salt
  • 12 Cup Unsalted Butter, Cold and Cubed
  • 1 Cup Buttermilk
  • 1/4 Cup Grated Cheddar Cheese

Steps

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a large skillet or ovenproof pan, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the chopped onion, carrots, celery, and garlic. Sauté until the vegetables become tender, about 5-7 minutes.
  3. Sprinkle the flour over the sautéed vegetables and stir to form a roux. Cook for a minute or two to remove the raw taste of the flour.
  4. Gradually pour in the chicken broth and whole milk, stirring constantly to create a smooth sauce. Bring the mixture to a simmer, and let it cook until it thickens, about 5-7 minutes.
  5. Stir in the cooked chicken, frozen peas, frozen corn, dried thyme, salt, and black pepper. Cook for an additional 2-3 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together. Remove from heat.
  6. For the biscuit topping, in a separate mixing bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, baking soda, and salt. Add the cold, cubed butter and use a pastry cutter or your fingers to work it into the dry ingredients until you have a coarse, crumbly mixture.
  7. Pour in the buttermilk and gently stir until just combined. Do not overmix.
  8. Drop spoonfuls of the biscuit dough onto the chicken mixture in the skillet, spreading them out evenly. If desired, sprinkle grated cheddar cheese on top of the biscuit dough.
  9. Place the skillet in the preheated oven and bake for 25-30 minutes, or until the biscuits are golden brown and cooked through.
  10. Once done, remove the chicken cobbler from the oven and let it cool slightly. Garnish with chopped fresh parsley.
